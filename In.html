About the Lab

The GEODE Lab at Northern Arizona University conducts interdisciplinary research combining remote sensing, geospatial analysis, machine learning, and ecological field methods.

Our research focuses on climate-driven ecosystem transformations, biodiversity monitoring, carbon dynamics, and land-use change. We collaborate with government agencies and global institutions to inform environmental policy and decision-making.

The lab integrates expertise in environmental science, computer science, and spatial statistics to address critical questions about Earth system processes and sustainability in the Anthropocene.

Research

The GEODE Lab conducts cutting-edge research using remote sensing, geospatial analysis, and ecosystem modeling to understand how Earth's systems respond to climate and human pressures.

Our themes include vegetation dynamics, biodiversity monitoring, land surface phenology, and carbon cycling. Our work informs policy and conservation strategies worldwide.
